- **Problem:** "Intelligent Design" (Manual Prompt Engineering) is brittle and slow. The user cannot foresee every edge case. A system designed from the top-down often fails when it meets the chaotic reality of the bottom-up.

- **How to evolve a prompt?** It takes a base prompt (The Ancestor) and creates 10 mutations (changing tone, adding constraints, shifting focus). It runs them in parallel (`['fast mode'(-ing) BPMS]`) and keeps the one that yields the best result.
- **How to handle diversity?** It maintains a "Gene Pool" of diverse traits to prevent "Inbreeding" (Over-fitting). It occasionally introduces "Wild Type" genes (Randomness) to spark innovation.

##### **What if...?**

- **What if a mutation is harmful?** The "Natural Selection" filter (User Feedback or Automated Testing) kills the variant before it can reproduce. It becomes a fossil in the logs.
- **What if the environment changes?** The system triggers a "Cambrian Explosion"—a rapid burst of high-mutation evolution to adapt to the new conditions (e.g., a new API update).
