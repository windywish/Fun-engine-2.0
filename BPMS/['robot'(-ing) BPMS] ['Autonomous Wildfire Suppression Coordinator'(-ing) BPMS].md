- **Wind:** Fire generates its own wind (convection); creates eerie howling through trees

**Visual Phenomenology (Through Smoke):**
- **Orange glow:** Through smoke, fire is a diffuse orange glow—amorphous, threatening
- **Spot fires:** Sudden bright points appearing (embers landed)—each is a new threat
- **Crown transitions:** Moment when surface fire "ladders" into canopy—sudden intensity increase, visually dramatic
- **Black-orange boundary:** The fire front—sharp delineation between unburned (black/green) and burning (orange/red)

**Chemical Phenomenology:**
- **Smoke density:** Measured in visibility distance and particulate concentration—thick smoke is claustrophobic, disorienting
- **Combustion gases:** CO levels indicate combustion efficiency—incomplete combustion (smoldering) creates toxic CO-rich smoke
- **Oxygen depletion:** Near large fires, oxygen levels drop (consumed by combustion)—robot sensors detect, humans would suffocate
- **Vegetation volatiles:** Different plants smell different when burning—sagebrush, pine, oak each have signature

**Temporal Phenomenology:**

**Fire Time is Non-Linear:**
- **Slow periods:** Fire smoldering in heavy fuel—hours of minimal growth
- **Explosive runs:** Sudden wind shift or terrain funneling—fire doubles in minutes
- **Diurnal patterns:** Fires typically intensify afternoon (hottest, driest, windiest), calm at night
- **Critical moments:** Transitions when fire behavior regime shifts—these are the moments that matter most
