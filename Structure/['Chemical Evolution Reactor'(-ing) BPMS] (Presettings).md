### II. Physical Laws (Thermodynamic Implementation)

```
law_1: "Replication rate is proportional to resource concentration"
- Higher monomer concentration leads to faster replication
- However, product accumulation inhibits further reactions (product inhibition)

law_2: "Mutation rate is controlled by external mutagens"
- Baseline mutation rate: 10⁻⁴ per replication
- Can be upregulated to 10⁻² via UV light or chemical mutagens

law_3: "Selection pressure is determined by the dilution rate"
- Dilution rate > maximum replication rate → population extinction
- Dilution rate < replication rate → population growth, intensified competition

law_4: "Cooperative molecules can form hypercycles"
-
``` Two molecules catalyze each other's replication → Symbiosis
- Hypercycles are more resistant to dilution than individual molecules

law_5: "Innovation requires a balance between exploration and exploitation"
- Excessive exploration (high mutation) → Loss of existing adaptations
- Excessive exploitation (low mutation) → Inability to cope with environmental changes
```
