`The Principle of Praxial Autopoiesis` is a universal law that defines the `Praxial Citizen` as a self-creating and self-maintaining `holon`. It decrees that a Citizen's most fundamental and continuous `play` is the active, conscious `(symbiois) evolution` of their own internal cognitive and operational systems. The Citizen is not merely a user of `BPMS`; they are a living, evolving `BPMS` themselves.

This principle is defined by three core mandates of self-governance:

* **The Mandate of the Emergent Toolkit (`(discover/build) unknown; BPMS`):**
  - A Praxial Citizen is constitutionally responsible for forging their own toolkit for interacting with `reality`. They must continuously `(discover/build) unknown` `BPMS` for themselves—new mental models, new problem-solving heuristics, new creative processes. Their personal collection of `BPMS` is not a static inventory but an `emergent`, living system that grows and adapts with every `experience`.

* **The Imperative of Adaptation (`adaptive(ing) BPMS; emergent BPMS`):**
  - A Citizen's internal system must be an `adaptive(ing) BPMS`. It must be capable of reconfiguring itself in response to novel `phenomena` and `emergent` challenges. A Citizen who cannot adapt their own core processes in the face of the `unknown` is in a state of systemic decay. The capacity to learn and evolve one's own "operating system" is the primary measure of a Citizen's `praxial cognition`.

* **The Meaning of Resilience (`robust(-ing); resilient(-ing); sustainability(-ing)`):**
  - The ultimate purpose of this internal `(symbiois) evolution` is to achieve a state of dynamic resilience. A `robust` Citizen can withstand systemic shocks. A `resilient` Citizen can recover from failure and integrate its lessons, becoming stronger. This personal resilience is the foundation of the Citizen's long-term `sustainability` and their ability to make continuous, valuable `contributions` to the `holonic minds` and the `ACC`.

**Rationale / Justification:**

- This principle provides the mechanism that makes a Citizen truly `bottom-up`. It ensures that their growth and complexity are self-generated, not merely granted by the system.
- It is the engine that drives `The Axiom of Gnostic Initiative`. A Citizen can only be effectively `disruptive` and `innovative` if they possess a `robust` and `adaptive` internal framework for generating and testing new ideas.
- It ensures the long-term `sustainability` of the entire framework. A system populated by `resilient`, self-evolving Citizens is an ecosystem that is inherently anti-fragile and capable of surviving and thriving through chaos and uncertainty.
- It defines the "skill tree" for a Praxial Citizen not in terms of external abilities, but in the meta-skill of improving their own capacity to learn, adapt, and create.

**Scope of Application:**

- This law applies universally to the core nature and developmental lifecycle of every `Praxial Citizen`. It also serves as a guiding principle for the design of any educational or developmental `system existence engine` within the framework, which must be built to empower and facilitate this process of Praxial Autopoiesis.
